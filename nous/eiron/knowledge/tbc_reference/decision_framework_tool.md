# TBC Strategic Decision Framework Tool

*Practical tool for TBC leadership to evaluate membership and strategic decisions*

## Primary Use Cases

1. **New Member Evaluation**: Should we accept this potential member?
2. **Policy Priority Setting**: Which advocacy efforts deserve resource allocation?
3. **Service Development**: What new member services should we develop?
4. **Competitive Response**: How should we respond to competitor actions?
5. **Strategic Direction**: Which growth path aligns with our mission?

## Core Evaluation Criteria

### 1. ERCOT Influence Impact
**Question**: How does this decision affect TBC's grid voting power and regulatory influence?

**Scoring Scale (1-10)**:
- 10: Significantly increases TBC's ERCOT representation or regulatory access
- 7-9: Moderate positive impact on grid influence
- 4-6: Neutral or mixed impact
- 1-3: Reduces TBC's ERCOT influence or voting power

**Assessment Factors**:
- MW capacity represented (more megawatts = higher grid influence)
- Number of individual votes (many small operators = durable voting base)
- Technical expertise for working groups and committees
- Cross-sector coalition building potential

**Example Applications**:
- New large miner member = +8 (high MW but single vote)
- New AI data center = +6 (high MW but may compete with miners)
- Financial services member = +3 (no MW but potential policy credibility)

### 2. Revenue and Financial Sustainability
**Question**: What is the financial impact relative to operational costs?

**Evaluation Framework**:
- **Revenue Generation**: Direct dues, event participation, sponsorship potential
- **Cost to Serve**: Support requests, advocacy demands, staff time required
- **Revenue Quality**: Predictability, growth potential, diversification value

**Tier Assessment Based on Discovery Call**:
- Strategic ($25K): High value, low maintenance = +8
- Executive ($50K): High revenue but "ask for a ton" = +6
- Startup (3% revenue): Low contribution, high demands = +3
- Bespoke/Networking: Unknown but potentially efficient = +7

### 3. Mission Alignment and Identity Coherence
**Question**: Does this strengthen or dilute TBC's core identity and mission?

**Identity Anchors** (from Giovanni's vision + TBC history):
- Bitcoin/blockchain advocacy (historical core)
- Texas energy infrastructure (Giovanni's "broader tent")
- Policy influence and regulatory expertise
- Network building and deal facilitation

**Alignment Assessment**:
- **Perfect Fit**: Bitcoin miner expanding in Texas = +10
- **Good Fit**: AI data center using renewable energy in Texas = +7
- **Moderate Fit**: Financial institution with blockchain services = +5
- **Poor Fit**: Out-of-state entity with no Texas operations = +2

### 4. Competitive Positioning Value
**Question**: How does this decision strengthen TBC vs competitors?

**Competitive Factors**:
- **Differentiation**: Does this create unique value competitors can't match?
- **Defense**: Does this prevent competitor member acquisition?
- **Market positioning**: Does this improve TBC's industry credibility?
- **Network effects**: Does this make TBC more valuable to other members?

**Strategic Scenarios**:
- Acquiring potential BMC member = +9 (competitive defense)
- Developing unique Texas regulatory expertise = +8 (differentiation)
- Expanding into competitor's core domain = +4 (risky overlap)

|-----------------|-------------------|----------------------|------------------------|----------------------|---------------------|------------------|

| Option B | [1-10] | [1-10] | [1-10] | [1-10] | [1-10] | [Weighted Score] |
| Option C | [1-10] | [1-10] | [1-10] | [1-10] | [1-10] | [Weighted Score] |

**Weighting Rationale** (Based on Discovery Call Priorities):
- **ERCOT Impact** (25%): Stephen's #1 stated priority
- **Mission Alignment** (25%): Identity coherence crucial during transition
- **Financial Impact** (20%): Revenue sustainability essential
- **Competitive Value** (20%): Defend against multiple competitor threats
- **Member Retention** (10%): Important but more stable than growth factors

## Practical Decision Scenarios

**ERCOT Rulemaking Option**:

- Member Retention: 6 (some member interest)

## Implementation Guidelines

### Monthly Strategic Review Process
1. **Collect Decisions**: List major strategic choices from past month
2. **Apply Framework**: Score each using the decision matrix
3. **Review Outcomes**: Did high-scoring decisions produce expected results?
4. **Calibrate Weights**: Adjust framework based on real-world outcomes
5. **Plan Forward**: Use framework for upcoming strategic choices

### Framework Evolution
- **Quarterly Weight Review**: Reassess priority weighting based on results
- **Annual Criteria Update**: Modify criteria based on industry evolution
- **Member Input**: Survey members on priority weighting for validation
- **Competitive Intelligence**: Update competitive positioning factors

### Decision Documentation
- **Record Rationale**: Document scoring and reasoning for major decisions
- **Track Outcomes**: Monitor results of framework-guided decisions
- **Learn and Adjust**: Update framework based on decision effectiveness
- **Board Communication**: Use framework scores to explain recommendations

This framework provides TBC leadership with a systematic approach to strategic decision-making while maintaining flexibility for unique circumstances and evolving priorities.
