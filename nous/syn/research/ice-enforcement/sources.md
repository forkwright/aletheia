# ICE Enforcement — Source Index

## Operation Metro Surge
- **Start**: December 4, 2025 (announced), escalated January 6, 2026
- **Scale**: "Largest immigration enforcement operation ever carried out" — DHS
- **Location**: Minneapolis-St. Paul, expanded to all Minnesota
- **Personnel**: 2,700 agents deployed; 700 withdrawn Feb 4; 2,000 remain
- **Arrests**: 3,000+ people
- **Source**: Wikipedia (well-sourced), Guardian, NYT, AP

## Court Order Violations
- Federal judge Patrick Schiltz: ICE violated at least **96 court orders** in Minnesota since Jan 1, 2026
- Admin ignoring legal requirements to release people who post bond
- 5th Circuit backed mass detention policy, contradicting lower courts
- Source: Wikipedia (cited), AP

## Community Impact
- Families barricaded in homes for weeks (one man, 29 days)
- Schools shifted to remote learning
- Neighbors delivering groceries to those afraid to leave
- Whistle networks alerting neighborhoods to ICE presence
- Economy and civil society "disrupted" per multiple sources
- Source: Guardian 2026-02-09 (families trapped), Wikipedia

## Legal Challenges
- MN AG Keith Ellison + Minneapolis + St. Paul filed suit against DHS
- NY AG Letitia James deployed legal observers in purple vests
- Federal judges finding bond requirements ignored
- Trump threatened Insurrection Act against Minnesota protesters
- Source: Multiple

## Broader Enforcement (National)
- Deportations quadrupled — UCLA report, Feb 4
- ICE street arrests up elevenfold
- Target was 1,200-1,500/day; actual under 600/day and declining
- ICE detention system on track for 100,000+ beds in 2026
- Source: Various, compiled from search results

## Protest Movement
- Jan 30 "National Shutdown" — nationwide coordinated anti-ICE protests
- AP reported "broad cultural revolt" forming
- Demands: ICE abolition, body cameras, warrant requirements, federal withdrawal
- DHS calls protesters "agitators" helping criminals evade deportation
