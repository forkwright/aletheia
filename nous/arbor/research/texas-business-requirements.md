# Texas Tree Service/Arborist Business Requirements

*Research compiled on January 29, 2026*

## Executive Summary

Starting a legitimate tree service/arborist business in Texas requires careful attention to business formation, insurance coverage, certifications, and tax obligations. Texas has relatively business-friendly regulations but specific requirements for tree care services due to safety and liability concerns.

## 1. LLC Formation in Texas

### Process
1. **Choose a unique business name** that complies with Texas naming requirements
   - Must include "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company"
   - Check availability through Texas SOS website
   - Consider registering an assumed name (DBA) if operating under different name

2. **File Certificate of Formation**
   - Submit to Texas Secretary of State
   - Can be filed online, by mail, or in person
   - Processing time: 5-7 business days (standard), 2-3 days (expedited)

3. **Obtain Federal EIN**
   - Apply through IRS website (free)
   - Required for tax purposes and opening business bank accounts

4. **Create Operating Agreement**
   - Not required by law but highly recommended
   - Defines ownership, management, and operating procedures

### Costs
- **State filing fee**: $308 (as of 2024)
- **Expedited processing**: Additional $25
- **Registered agent**: $100-300/year if using service
- **Operating agreement**: $200-1,000 if professionally drafted
- **Total estimated cost**: $500-1,500

### Benefits for Tree Service Business
- **Limited liability protection**: Personal assets protected from business debts/lawsuits
- **Tax flexibility**: Choose how to be taxed (sole proprietorship, partnership, S-Corp, C-Corp)
- **Professional credibility**: Enhanced reputation with customers and suppliers
- **Easier financing**: Banks prefer lending to formal entities
- **Operational continuity**: Business can continue beyond individual owners

**Recommendation**: Form an LLC. The liability protection is crucial for tree services due to inherent risks of the work.

## 2. Insurance Requirements

### Required Insurance
1. **Workers' Compensation Insurance**
   - **Required by law** if you have employees
   - Texas Labor Code Chapter 406
   - Can self-insure if you meet specific requirements
   - **Cost**: $2,000-8,000/year depending on payroll and risk factors

2. **Commercial Auto Insurance**
   - Required if using vehicles for business
   - Must meet Texas minimum liability requirements
   - **Minimum**: $30,000 per person, $60,000 per accident, $25,000 property damage

### Highly Recommended Insurance
1. **General Liability Insurance**
   - Covers third-party injury and property damage
   - **Recommended coverage**: $1-2 million per occurrence
   - **Cost**: $1,200-3,000/year for tree services

2. **Professional Liability/Errors & Omissions**
   - Covers mistakes in professional services
   - Important for tree assessment and consulting
   - **Cost**: $800-2,000/year

3. **Commercial Property Insurance**
   - Covers equipment, tools, and business property
   - Include equipment coverage for expensive tree care tools
   - **Cost**: $500-1,500/year

4. **Umbrella Policy**
   - Additional liability coverage above other policies
   - **Recommended**: $1-5 million coverage
   - **Cost**: $200-600/year per million in coverage

**Total estimated insurance costs**: $5,000-15,000/year

**Recommendation**: Obtain comprehensive coverage. Tree work involves significant risks to people and property. Many clients will require proof of insurance before hiring.

## 3. Licensing and Certification Requirements

### State Licensing
**Texas does NOT require state licensing for tree service providers.** However, certain activities may require permits:

1. **Pesticide Application**
   - If applying pesticides, need Texas Dept. of Agriculture license
   - Commercial Pesticide Applicator License required
   - **Process**: Complete training, pass exam, pay fees
   - **Cost**: $75 initial license, $60 renewal every 5 years

2. **Electrical Work**
   - If working near power lines, may need electrical contractor license
   - Coordinate with utility companies for line clearance

### Professional Certifications (Recommended)
1. **International Society of Arboriculture (ISA) Certified Arborist**
   - Industry gold standard certification
   - **Requirements**: 3 years experience OR degree + 2 years + exam
   - **Cost**: $300-500 for exam and application
   - **Benefits**: Professional credibility, higher rates, insurance discounts

2. **Tree Care Industry Association (TCIA) Accreditation**
   - Company-level accreditation
   - **Requirements**: Safety programs, insurance, trained personnel
   - **Cost**: $500-2,000 depending on company size
   - **Benefits**: Marketing advantage, better insurance rates

3. **Texas Board of Professional Engineers**
   - Required only if offering engineering services (structural assessments)
   - Most tree services don't need this

### Municipal Requirements
- Check local city/county requirements
- Some areas require business licenses or permits
- **Cost**: Typically $25-200/year

**Recommendation**: Pursue ISA Certified Arborist credentials. While not legally required, it significantly enhances credibility and allows premium pricing.

## 4. Tax Considerations

### Federal Taxes
1. **LLC Tax Elections**
   - **Default**: Disregarded entity (sole proprietorship tax treatment)
   - **Option**: S-Corp election to potentially save on self-employment taxes
   - **When beneficial**: Annual profit >$60,000-80,000

2. **Quarterly Estimated Taxes**
   - Required if expecting to owe >$1,000 in taxes
   - Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15

### State Taxes
1. **Texas Franchise Tax**
   - **Threshold**: Only if annual receipts >$1.18 million
   - **Rate**: 0.375% on taxable margin for most entities
   - Most new tree services won't meet threshold initially

2. **Sales Tax**
   - **Tree removal**: Generally not subject to sales tax (service)
   - **Tree planting/products**: Subject to sales tax
   - **Registration**: Required if selling tangible goods
   - **Rate**: 6.25% state + local rates

### Employment Taxes
1. **FICA/Medicare**: 15.3% on wages (7.65% employer + employee each)
2. **FUTA**: 0.6% on first $7,000 per employee
3. **SUTA**: Texas unemployment tax rate varies (currently 0.31%-6.31%)

### Business Deductions
- Equipment and tools
- Vehicle expenses
- Insurance premiums
- Certification costs
- Training and education
- Business meals (50%)
- Professional memberships

**Recommendation**: Consider S-Corp election once profitable. Consult with CPA for optimal tax strategy.

## 5. Additional Requirements

### OSHA Compliance
- Follow OSHA safety standards for tree care operations
- Develop safety programs and training
- Required for companies with employees

### Environmental Regulations
- Protected tree ordinances (varies by city)
- Migratory Bird Treaty Act compliance
- Proper disposal of tree waste

### Bonding
- Some commercial clients require surety bonds
- **Cost**: 1-3% of bond amount annually
- **Typical amount**: $10,000-50,000
