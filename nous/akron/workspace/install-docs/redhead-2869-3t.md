## Critical Torque Specifications

### Pitman Arm Torque
**Per RedHead FAQ**: 185 ft-lbs (for 1993-2002 Dodge 2500/3500)
- **DO NOT use anti-seize** - splines are tapered for perfect connection
- **DO NOT hammer or press** pitman arm
- **Use torque wrench only** - NO IMPACT TOOLS
- Pitman arm should seat with some splines still exposed on sector shaft

## Installation Procedures

### Pre-Installation
1. **Verify Ratio**: Count turns lock-to-lock on current steering wheel
   - 3-3.5 turns = 15:1 ratio (2869-3T correct)
   - 4-4.5 turns = 18:1 ratio (need 2869-4T instead)

2. **Fluid Specification**: 
   - Use manufacturer-recommended fluid (typically Dexron 3 ATF)
   - RedHead tests with Dexron 3 ATF before shipping

### Installation Steps
1. Remove old steering box
2. Install new RedHead box with frame bolts to 95 ft-lbs (verify spec)
3. Install pitman arm - DO NOT use anti-seize
4. Torque pitman arm nut to 185 ft-lbs with torque wrench
5. Connect steering linkage
6. Fill and bleed system (see bleeding procedure below)

### Air Bleeding Procedure (CRITICAL)
**Per RedHead FAQ**:
1. Fill system with fluid
2. Raise front wheels off ground - **DO NOT START ENGINE**
3. Work steering wheel lock-to-lock 15-20 times
4. Let sit AT LEAST 2 hours (overnight best)
5. Top off reservoir
6. Work steering wheel 7-10 times more
7. Have helper at reservoir with fluid ready
8. Start engine - **immediately top off fluid as level drops**
9. Must maintain fluid level or will suck air back in

## Potential Issues & Gotchas

### Installation Gotchas
1. **Pitman Arm Removal**: Most challenging part - consider purchasing new pitman arm from RedHead to avoid removal
2. **Anti-Seize**: DO NOT USE on pitman arm splines
3. **Air Bleeding**: Critical - must follow procedure exactly or steering will be poor
4. **Centering**: Box ships centered - maintain this during installation

### Break-In Considerations
1. **200-mile restriction** on full lock-to-lock (source: your knowledge - verify with RedHead)
2. **Initial settling**: Some users report needing adjustment after break-in period
3. **Fluid monitoring**: Check fluid level frequently during break-in
