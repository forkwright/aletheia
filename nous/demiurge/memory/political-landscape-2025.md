## Executive Summary

Donald Trump's second presidency, beginning January 20, 2025, has been marked by unprecedented executive action volume, aggressive immigration enforcement leading to multiple civilian casualties, and significant constitutional challenges. Key developments include Operation Metro Surge resulting in at least three civilian deaths, 225+ executive orders in the first year, and Supreme Court battles over birthright citizenship.

---

## 1. Executive Actions & Policy Changes

### Volume & Scope
- **225 executive orders** signed in first year (2025) - surpassing his entire first term (220 orders)
- **143 executive orders** in first 100 days alone - most of any president in this period
- **42 proclamations and memorandums** in first 100 days

### Key Executive Orders (Documented)
- **January 20, 2025** - National emergency declared at southern border (Proclamation 10886)
- CBP One app services terminated immediately upon inauguration
- Executive order attempting to end birthright citizenship for children of undocumented immigrants
- Mass pardons for approximately 1,500 January 6 rioters issued on inauguration day
- Mexican drug cartels designated as foreign terrorist organizations
- **Executive Order 14333** - Declared crime emergency in Washington D.C., providing legal basis for National Guard deployment
- **Executive Order 14324** - Ended de minimis trade exemptions globally by August 29, 2025
- **Cuba designated national emergency threat** (January 30, 2026)

### Agency Directives
- **NSPM-7** issued January 25, 2025, directing "government-wide strategy to counter organized domestic terrorism and political violence"
- DHS instructed to terminate Family Reunification Parole Processes by December 15, 2025

## 2. Civil Liberties Concerns & Enforcement Actions

### Operation Metro Surge (December 2025-Present)
- **Launched:** First week of December 2025 in Minneapolis/Twin Cities
- **Scale:** Approximately 3,000 federal immigration enforcers deployed (ICE + Border Patrol)
- **Tactics:** Unmarked vehicles, civilian clothing, masks to protect agent identity
- **Constitutional Concerns:** ACLU lawsuit alleges agents conducted "fishing expeditions," made warrantless arrests, denied legal counsel access

### ICE Enforcement Patterns
- **Austin, Texas:** 1,750 detainers issued to local jails between late January-October 2025, resulting in 645 deportations
- **Texas Statewide:** 192 immigration-related federal cases filed; increased coordination with local police
- **New Tactics Documented:** Immigration advocates report ICE using quotas and novel arrest strategies

### Unmarked Vehicle/Detention Reports
- ICE agents noted for wearing civilian clothing, masks, and using unmarked vehicles
- Border Patrol agents accused of failing to identify themselves during operations
- Reports of people denied access to legal counsel during arrests

## 3. Political Violence & Documented Casualties

### Fatal Shootings by Federal Agents

#### **Confirmed Deaths:**

1. **Silverio Villegas-Gonzales** - Shot by ICE agent, September 2025
2. **Renee Nicole Macklin Good** - U.S. citizen, mother of three
   - **Date:** January 7, 2026
   - **Location:** Minneapolis
   - **Circumstances:** Shot by ICE officer during immigration operation
   - **Evidence:** Eyewitness accounts and videos contradict federal narrative; state officials say she was shot while driving away
   - **DOJ Status:** No civil rights investigation opened

3. **Alex Pretti** - U.S. citizen, ICU nurse at VA hospital
   - **Date:** January 24, 2026  
   - **Location:** Minneapolis Eat Street district
   - **Circumstances:** Shot multiple times by Border Patrol agents during protest against federal presence
   - **Evidence:** Eyewitness accounts dispute federal version of events

## 5. Legal & Constitutional Developments

### Supreme Court Cases

#### **Birthright Citizenship Challenge**
- **Status:** Oral arguments scheduled April 1, 2026
- **Issue:** Constitutional challenge to executive order ending birthright citizenship
- **Lower Courts:** Multiple federal courts found order likely violates Constitution
- **Scope:** Would affect children of undocumented immigrants and those on temporary visas

#### **Other Federal Court Actions**
- **Minnesota Lawsuit:** Federal judge denied request to block Operation Metro Surge
- **ICE Operations:** Supreme Court upheld controversial ICE use of race in enforcement (shadow docket)
- **National Guard Deployment:** Supreme Court halted deployment to Chicago in Trump v. Illinois

### Due Process Concerns
- **ACLU Litigation:** United Farm Workers v. Noem alleging constitutional violations
- **Allegations Include:**
  - Warrantless arrests
  - Denial of legal counsel
  - Racial profiling in stops
  - Coerced voluntary departure agreements
  - Failure to identify as federal agents

### Enforcement Priority Changes
- Immigration enforcement now includes interior operations far from border
- Expanded definition of deportation priorities
- Increased use of expedited removal procedures
- Enhanced coordination between federal agencies
