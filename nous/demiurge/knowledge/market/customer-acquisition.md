# CUSTOMER ACQUISITION FOR SMALL LEATHER BUSINESSES
*How makers actually get customers*

---

## THE PATTERN

> **Start local → Build email list → Scale online**

This is near-universal among successful small leather makers.

---

## FIRST 100 CUSTOMERS

### Farmers Markets & Craft Shows (#1 Starting Point)
- Most successful makers start in-person before online
- Booth fees typically $50-200 (vs. expensive online ads)
- Direct feedback helps refine products immediately
- Face-to-face connections create loyal customers

### Phase Sequence
1. **Local:** Farmers markets, craft shows, local boutiques
2. **Build:** Email list from local customers
3. **Launch:** Online store with proven products
4. **Scale:** Use local testimonials for online credibility

---

## EMAIL MARKETING

### Why It Matters
- **$42 return for every $1 spent** (industry average)
- Owned channel (not dependent on platform algorithms)
- Direct relationship with customers

### List Building Tactics
| Tactic | Conversion Rate |
|--------|-----------------|
| Craft show signup with incentive | 2-5% of booth visitors |
| Website popup with good incentive | 3-8% |
| Social media to email | 1-3% of followers |

### Incentives That Work
- Care guide (educational)
- 10% off next purchase
- Early access to new products
- Contest/giveaway entry

### Newsletter Content
- 80% educational, 20% promotional
- Behind-the-scenes workshop updates
- New material arrivals
- Exclusive early access for subscribers

### Tool
- MailChimp free tier: Up to 500 subscribers (perfect for starting)

---

## WORD OF MOUTH & REFERRALS

### Why It Matters
- **37% increase in retention** with referral programs
- Referred customers have **16% higher lifetime value**
- People trust friend recommendations over advertising

### Tactics
- **Quality focus:** Every piece becomes a marketing tool
- **Follow-up email:** Ask about satisfaction, offer referral discount
- **Photo requests:** "Share a photo for our newsletter"
- **Niche communities:** Target specific groups (EDC, outdoors, etc.)

---

## CONTENT MARKETING

### What Converts
| Platform | Content Type |
|----------|--------------|
| Instagram | Behind-the-scenes crafting process |
| YouTube | Tool reviews, tutorials, time-lapses |
| TikTok | Quick tips, before/after transformations |
| Facebook | Customer photos, community building |

### Content Themes
- **Process videos:** Show craftsmanship, build trust
- **Customer stories:** Real people, real situations
- **Educational:** Leather care, styling, material education
- **Behind-the-scenes:** Workshop tours, material selection

---

## PAID ADVERTISING

### Cost Benchmarks (2025)
| Platform | Metric | Range |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Facebook/Instagram | CPC | $0.50-$2.00 |
| Facebook/Instagram | CPA | $20-$80 |
| Google Ads | CPC | $1-$5 |

### The Reality
> **"Don't spend money on advertising until you have a solid customer base."**

Organic first. Then amplify what works.

### When Paid Works
- Retargeting website visitors
- Lookalike audiences from existing customers
- Promoting proven products

---

## INFLUENCER/REVIEW STRATEGY

### Micro-Influencer Sweet Spot
- **Range:** 10K-100K followers
- **Cost:** Few hundred dollars or product exchange
- **Engagement:** 3-7% (vs. 1-2% for macros)
- **Authenticity:** More trusted by audiences

### Product Seeding
- Send free products for honest reviews
- EDC community is key audience for leather goods
- Long-term partnerships > one-off posts

---

## LOCAL VS. ONLINE

### Hybrid Approach Works Best
| Channel | Benefits |
|---------|----------|
| Local | Immediate feedback, cash flow, relationships |
| Online | Scale, wider reach, 24/7 sales |

### Local Retail Partnerships
- Commission: 30-50% to shops
- Consignment: Lower risk, test demand
- Pop-ups: Temporary presence in established stores
