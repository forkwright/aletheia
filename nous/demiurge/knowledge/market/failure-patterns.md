# SMALL LEATHER BUSINESS FAILURE PATTERNS
*What kills small makers — and how to avoid it*

---

## THE CORE INSIGHT

> **"Most leather business failures are business failures, not craft failures."**

Great craft + broken business = failure. The pattern is predictable and preventable.

---

## #1 KILLER: UNDERPRICING

### The Hobbyist Trap
- Forum asks "what should I charge for this 2-hour wallet with $10 materials?"
- Crowd suggests $20
- **Doesn't even cover materials, let alone labor**

### Correct Pricing Formula
```
Wholesale = (Materials + Labor) × 2
Retail = Wholesale × 2

Example:
$35 materials + $60 labor = $95 cost
$95 × 2 = $190 wholesale
$190 × 2 = $380 retail
```

### Pricing Mistakes
- Only counting leather + hardware (ignoring time, wastage, overhead)
- "I'll start low and raise prices later" (you won't)
- Asking Facebook groups (they're not your target customer)
- "What would I pay?" (your budget ≠ target customer's budget)
- Cringing when stating prices → immediate discounts

---

## #2 KILLER: NO CUSTOMER DEFINITION

### The Problem
- "Everyone who likes leather" = no one
- Generic messaging attracts no one specifically
- Can't explain why $100 wallet differs from $20 factory version

### The Fix
- Define specific persona BEFORE making anything
- Not "leather lovers" but "X profession who values Y"
- Clear value proposition for that specific person

---

## #3 KILLER: PLATFORM DEPENDENCE

### The Pattern
- Etsy-only syndrome
- "If I post on Instagram, they will buy"
- Building audience on platforms you don't own

### The Reality
- Platform algorithms change
- Fees increase
- Competition floods in
- You have no owned asset

### The Fix
- Email list from day one
- Own website as foundation
- Platforms as traffic sources, not home base

---

## #4 KILLER: ONE-PERSON BOTTLENECK

### The Pattern (from Leatherworker.net)
One person does: cutting, stitching, customer service, shipping, marketing

**Chain reaction:**
1. Lead times stretch beyond 2-3 weeks
2. Communication falters during busy periods
3. Quality drops under pressure
4. Reviews decline
5. Word-of-mouth dies
6. Business contracts

### The Fix
- Systems BEFORE you need them
- Batch processing (not one item at a time)
- Standardized patterns
- Help before you're drowning

---

## #5 KILLER: SUPPLY CHAIN VULNERABILITY

### Real Example
> "I just took in ten orders for notebooks for Christmas and my paper source just went bust. Now I am stuck... Deposits have been given already."

### The Pattern
- Single-source suppliers
- No buffer inventory
- Long lead times not communicated
- Quality control issues with new suppliers

### The Fix
- Multiple suppliers for critical materials
- Buffer stock for core materials
- Relationships with backups BEFORE crisis

---

## #6 KILLER: WRONG PRODUCT FOCUS

### Product Line Problems
- Generic product mix (wallets, keychains, belts with nothing unique)
- Too many SKUs (spreading thin)
- No signature item (nothing they're known for)
- Style confusion (mixing tactical, Western, luxury, minimalist)

### The Fix
- One signature product first
- Master it completely
- Expand only after it's working

---

## #7 KILLER: MARKET SATURATION DENIAL

### The Reality
> "Competition is tough. My $100 items can't compete with a crappy $20 wallet with 'world's best dad' laser printed on it."

> "There are far too many leather shops in Arizona as it is!"

### The Pattern
- Too late to generic markets
- Entering mature platforms without differentiation
- Thinking quality alone will win

### The Fix
- Differentiation strategy required
- Specific niche, not general market
- Story and positioning, not just quality

---

## OPERATIONAL FAILURES

| Failure | Pattern | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| No standardized patterns | Every piece different | Templates, jigs, consistency |
| Inefficient workspace | Poor layout | Optimize flow |
| No batch processing | One at a time | Batch similar operations |
| Ad-hoc scheduling | Chaos | System for multiple orders |
| Cash flow gaps | Irregular income | Financial cushion |
| Inventory before validation | Built stock that doesn't sell | Validate first |

- ✅ Quality materials with story (Hermann Oak, Abbey England)
