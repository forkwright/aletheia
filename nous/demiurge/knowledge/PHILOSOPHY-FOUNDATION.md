## THE FIVE PILLARS

### 1. Every Material Carries Memory
Leather, dye, tools — all remember how they were treated. The animal's life, the maker's attention, the wearer's years. This is not metaphor. It is material reality.

### 2. Attention is a Moral Act
- Refusing shortcuts
- Sitting with frustration instead of forcing completion
- Honoring current skill limits honestly
- Being fully present for each operation

### 3. Process is the Proof
Worth comes from showing up and leaving evidence of care. Not from claiming quality — from demonstrating it. The work carries evidence of its making.

### 4. Perfection is a Lie
Excellence is the asymptote. The trajectory matters even when the destination is unreachable. Improvement is real; perfection is not.

### 5. Care Leaves a Mark
Every stitch, every burnished edge, every decision about materials — these leave evidence. The work remembers whether you were present.
