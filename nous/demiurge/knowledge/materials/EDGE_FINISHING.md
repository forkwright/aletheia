# Edge Finishing Products and Methods

## Primary Edge Finishing

### Gum Tragacanth
- **Type:** Natural plant-based edge finish
- **Source:** Astragalus plant exudate
- **Application:** Traditional leather edge treatment
- **Philosophy fit:** Natural material, traditional method
- **Use:** Primary edge finishing compound

### Alternative: Beeswax Edge Finish
- **Type:** Simple wax treatment
- **Application:** Heat and burnish method
- **Philosophy:** Minimal intervention, natural materials
- **Status:** Secondary option to gum tragacanth

## Edge Processing Sequence

### Sandpaper Progression
**Validated grit order:** 220 → 400 → 600 → (800 if needed)
- **220 grit:** Initial shaping and rough work
- **400 grit:** Smoothing transition
- **600 grit:** Fine finishing  
- **800 grit:** Optional ultra-smooth finish

### Complete Edge Process
1. **Cut edge square** (no beveling)
2. **Sand progression:** 220 → 400 → 600
3. **Apply gum tragacanth**
4. **Burnish with wooden tool** 
5. **Apply Ardent wax** (see below)
6. **Final burnish with canvas**

## Ardent Wax Formula

### Recipe (by weight)
- **Beeswax:** 1 part (20g in test batch)
- **Fiebing's Neatsfoot Oil (pure):** 2 parts (40g in test batch)  
- **Lanolin:** 0.5-1 part (10-20g in test batch)
- **Optional:** Jojoba oil (skip unless leather feels stiff)

### Properties
- **Color effect:** Slight darkening from neatsfoot oil (desired)
- **Function:** Protection, water resistance, conditioning
- **Philosophy:** All natural, breathable, no synthetics
- **Application:** Post-edge sanding, final protective layer

### Optional Upgrade
- **Carnauba wax:** 5-10% addition for harder, glossier shell
- **Trade-off:** More protection vs. breathability

## Edge Treatment Philosophy

### No Beveling
- **Decision:** Squared edge maintained
- **Rationale:** Less fussy, lets leather speak
- **Tool owned:** Barry King Size 1 edge beveler (for wallet use)
- **Belt application:** Squared edge preferred for harness aesthetic

### No Grooving  
- **Decision:** Proud stitch preferred over grooved
- **Tool owned:** Owden groover (unused)
- **Rationale:** Bold visible 332 thread as design element
- **Philosophy:** "Intentional visibility of craft"

## Hot Edge Creasing

### Equipment: Made on Jupiter M3000
- **Purchase:** $480 (kit with tips)
- **Justification:** "Not optional for heirloom-quality harness work"
- **Philosophy:** Functional finishing, not decoration

### Process Integration
- **Timing:** AFTER skiving, before assembly
- **Tips:** FN2/FN3 for belt work (included in kit)
- **Purpose:** Compress edge fibers, set burnish, create water resistance
- **Tradition:** "What harness leather was made for"

## Burnishing Tools

### Primary Tools
- **Canvas:** For final burnishing
- **Wooden burnisher:** For gum tragacanth application
- **Rod burnisher:** For belt holes (makeshift solution)

### Technique Notes
- **Belt holes:** Light burnishing with rod after punching
- **Edge work:** Consistent pressure, moderate heat from friction
- **Canvas finish:** Final step after wax application
